# Music Blocks Lesson Plans

A collection of structured, educator-ready lesson plans for integrating
Music Blocks into classroom settings. Plans are organized by level and
cover both music education and computational thinking concepts.

## Levels

### Beginner (Elementary, ages 6–10)

| # | Title | Music Concept | CS Concept |
|---|-------|---------------|------------|
| 1 | [Hello, Pitch!](./beginner/01-hello-pitch.md) | Pitch names, octaves | Sequences |
| 2 | [My First Rhythm](./beginner/02-my-first-rhythm.md) | Note values, beat | Loops |
| 3 | [Loud and Soft](./beginner/03-loud-and-soft.md) | Dynamics (volume) | Variables |

### Intermediate (Middle School, ages 11–14)

| # | Title | Music Concept | CS Concept |
|---|-------|---------------|------------|
| 4 | [Repeating Phrases](./intermediate/04-repeating-phrases.md) | Phrase repetition | Loops and iteration |
| 5 | [Call and Response](./intermediate/05-call-and-response.md) | Musical form | Actions / functions |
| 6 | [Major and Minor](./intermediate/06-major-and-minor.md) | Scales, mood | Conditionals |

### Advanced (High School / Conservatory, ages 15+)

| # | Title | Music Concept | CS Concept |
|---|-------|---------------|------------|
| 7 | [Transposition and Intervals](./advanced/07-transposition-intervals.md) | Intervals, transposition | Abstraction, modularity |

## Template

All lesson plans follow a consistent structure. See
[TEMPLATE.md](./TEMPLATE.md) for the blank template to use when
contributing new plans.

## Contributing

New lesson plans are welcome. Please:

1. Use [TEMPLATE.md](./TEMPLATE.md) as your starting point
2. Place the file in the correct level directory (`beginner/`, `intermediate/`, or `advanced/`)
3. Number files sequentially (e.g. `09-your-topic.md`)
4. Add an entry to the table above
5. Reference specific Music Blocks blocks, palettes, or widgets by name
6. Include at least one link to a runnable Music Blocks example where possible
