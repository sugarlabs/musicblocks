# Hello, Pitch!

**Level:** Beginner  
**Grade Range:** Grades 2–4 (ages 7–10)  
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Music Concept:** Pitch names (Do Re Mi), octaves  
**CS Concept:** Sequences — instructions executed in order  

---

## Overview

Students are introduced to musical pitch using the Pitch block in Music
Blocks. They build a simple ascending scale (Do–Re–Mi–Fa–Sol) by placing
Pitch blocks in sequence inside a Note Value block, learning that the
order of instructions matters — just like steps in a recipe.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

- **Music:** Name at least five pitches of the C major scale using solfège (Do Re Mi Fa Sol)
- **Music:** Understand that pitch describes how high or low a sound is
- **CS:** Explain what a sequence is and why order matters in a program
- **CS:** Build and run a simple block program in Music Blocks

---

## Prerequisite Knowledge

- No prior programming experience required
- Basic familiarity with a computer mouse or touchscreen

---

## Materials

- A device with a web browser and speakers/headphones
- [Music Blocks](https://musicblocks.sugarlabs.org) (no installation required)
- Blocks used: **Start** block, **Note Value** block, **Pitch** block (Pitch palette)
- Optional: printed solfège chart (Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Ti Do)

---

## Activity

### Step 1 — Open Music Blocks and explore (5 min)

1. Open [https://musicblocks.sugarlabs.org](https://musicblocks.sugarlabs.org) in a browser.
2. Click the **Play** button (triangle icon) to hear the default project.
3. Ask students: *"What did you hear? Was it high or low?"*

### Step 2 — Find the Pitch block (5 min)

1. Click the **Pitch** palette (the musical note icon on the left toolbar).
2. Drag a **Pitch** block onto the canvas.
3. Show students the two inputs on the Pitch block: the **note name** (e.g. Sol) and the **octave** (e.g. 4).
4. Click the note name dropdown and explore the options (Do, Re, Mi, Fa, Sol, La, Ti).

### Step 3 — Build a scale sequence (15 min)

1. Drag a **Start** block onto the canvas.
2. Inside the Start block, place a **Note Value** block (set to 1/4 — a quarter note).
3. Inside the Note Value block, place a **Pitch** block set to **Do, octave 4**.
4. Add four more Note Value + Pitch blocks below it: Re, Mi, Fa, Sol (all octave 4).
5. Click the **Run** button and listen.
6. Ask: *"What happened when we changed the order of the blocks?"* — swap two blocks and run again.

### Step 4 — Experiment (10 min)

1. Ask students to change one pitch to something unexpected (e.g. Ti instead of Mi).
2. Run the program and discuss: *"Did it sound like a scale? Why not?"*
3. Challenge: can they build a **descending** scale (Sol–Fa–Mi–Re–Do)?

### Step 5 — Share and reflect (10 min)

1. Pairs share their programs with the class.
2. Discuss: *"What is a sequence? Can you think of a sequence in your daily life?"*

---

## Discussion Prompts

- Why does the order of the Pitch blocks matter?
- What would happen if a recipe had its steps in the wrong order?
- Can you think of a song that goes up in pitch? Down in pitch?
- What is the difference between a high pitch and a low pitch?

---

## Assessment Criteria

- [ ] Student can name at least five solfège pitches in order
- [ ] Student successfully builds and runs a 5-note ascending scale
- [ ] Student can explain in their own words what a sequence is
- [ ] Student experiments with at least one modification to their program

---

## Extension Activities

- Build a full octave scale (Do to Do) using 8 Note Value blocks
- Try changing the octave number — what happens to the sound?
- Recreate the first 5 notes of a familiar song (e.g. "Mary Had a Little Lamb": Mi Re Do Re Mi Mi Mi)
- Explore the **Pitch-Time Matrix** widget to see pitches arranged in a grid
